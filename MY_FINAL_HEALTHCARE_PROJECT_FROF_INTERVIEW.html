Here’s a short and clear interpretation:¶

Accuracy (0.98): The logistic regression model is performing very well, correctly predicting high vs. low billing 98% of the time.

Most influential factor: Billing Amount (11.06) has by far the strongest positive coefficient, meaning it almost completely drives whether a patient is classified as “high billing.”

Other strong positive predictors:

Emergency admission (+2.05)

Chemotherapy medication (+1.50)

Older age (+0.96) These significantly increase the likelihood of being high billing.

Moderate contributors: Certain insurance providers, medications (e.g., statins, metformin), and conditions (cancer, heart disease) slightly increase the probability.

Negative predictors:

Urgent admission (–0.30)

Diabetes (–0.05)

Doctors (Dr. Smith, Dr. Patel) and Hypertension also slightly decrease the likelihood.

👉 In short: high billing is most strongly associated with higher billing amounts, emergency cases, chemotherapy, older age, and specific providers/medications. Urgent admissions and diabetes lower the odds.
